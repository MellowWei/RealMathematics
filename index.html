Abstract摘要 · problem / approach / contribution

Mathematics is presented here as a philosophical research artifact about the root action of mathematical cognition rather than as a replacement for formal mathematics or school mathematics. The problem it addresses is that modern mathematics education and many presentations of formal mathematics foreground deductive rules, symbolic manipulation, and proof procedures while leaving the prior act of recognition largely unnamed: the ability to recognize same-form across different appearances and different-nature beneath similar appearances. The approach is a four-axiom structure. M-0 defines mathematics as the method of recognizing same-form and different-nature, with deduction as its formalized product. M-1 treats mathematical operation as the deduction of induction. M-2, in its academic-audited form, states that at the recognition layer, categorial thinking is inductive logic. M-Ω closes the system by claiming that induction is both cause and effect: the attempted search for a ground outside induction already relies on inductive recognition. The contribution is a recognition-centered account of mathematics that can speak to mathematics education, categorical thinking, formalization, and the philosophy of induction. It does not claim to replace set theory, type theory, category theory, or other formal foundations; it names the recognition action that precedes and feeds them.

Dual Direction双向识别 · the core architecture
Same-Form + Different-Nature同型 + 异性 · the two directions of inductive recognition

Mathematics has two simultaneous directions of recognition. Mainstream education teaches neither; it teaches only the formal deduction that issues from them. Without these two directions, what remains is mechanical rule-following.

— Direction I · 方向一 —
识别同型Recognize Same-Form
Different-looking objects sharing identical relational structure. "X looks unlike Y, yet X and Y are structurally the same."

This is the merging direction of induction. It forms equivalence classes, categories, isomorphisms. It is what category theory formalizes.

The work: see beneath surfaces. A coffee cup and a torus share the same topological structure; that recognition is mathematics.
Example: Group structure of {0,1,2,3,4} under +mod5 is the same-form as {1,2,4,8,16} under ×mod31. The two groups look different; recognizing they are the same is mathematics.
— Direction II · 方向二 —
识别异性Recognize Different-Nature
Similar-looking objects with fundamentally different inner nature. "X looks like Y, yet X and Y are essentially different."

This is the separating direction of induction. It draws boundaries, finds counterexamples, refuses false identifications. "Different-nature" (异性) — not "different-form" (异型). The distinction is the nature (性), not the form (型).

The work: refuse false unity. A coffee cup and a sphere are topologically different; that distinction is mathematics.
Example: A real-valued continuous function and a measurable function look similar in domains/codomains. They are different in nature: continuity is local; measurability is global. Mathematics is the recognition of this distinction.
Both directions are necessary. Only same-form → false unifications (categorically collapsed nonsense). Only different-nature → no structural insight (endless separation without theory). The two directions in alternation = mathematics.

Reframes Hume's induction problem · 重构休谟归纳问题

Hume (1739) raised the induction problem: induction cannot be justified deductively. Later responses (Carnap, Popper, Bayesian inductivism, and related traditions) attempt to save, restrict, probabilize, or replace induction. This Mathematics reframes the problem by refusing the premise that induction must be justified by deduction: deduction is not independent of induction; deduction is the formalized output of induction (M-1). Therefore the question is no longer "how can deduction justify induction?" but "what recognition-action makes deduction possible?" This does not claim to refute every philosophical reading of Hume; it shifts the structural starting point.

休谟(1739)提出归纳问题:归纳不能被演绎合理化。后续回应传统(卡尔纳普、波普尔、贝叶斯归纳主义及相关传统)试图挽救、限制、概率化或替代归纳。本数学通过拒绝"归纳必须由演绎来证明"这一前提来重构问题:演绎不独立于归纳;演绎是归纳的形式化输出(M-1)。因此问题不再是"演绎如何证明归纳",而是"什么识别动作使演绎成为可能"。这不主张反驳休谟问题的所有哲学读法;它移动的是结构起点。
